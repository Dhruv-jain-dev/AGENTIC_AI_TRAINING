## Assignment

### Another Brick in the Wall

**Problem Statement:**

A customer requires **13 bricks**. Two workers, **John** and **Jack**, place bricks in iterations.

* In each iteration:

  * John places **n** bricks.
  * Jack places **2 × n** bricks.
* The value of **n** increases by 1 in every iteration.
* If the remaining bricks are fewer than the required number of bricks to be placed, only the remaining bricks are placed.

For the given input:

* Customer Requirement: **13 Bricks**

Iterations:

| Iteration | John | Jack          | Total Bricks |
| --------- | ---- | ------------- | ------------ |
| 1         | 1    | 2             | 3            |
| 2         | 2    | 4             | 9            |
| 3         | 3    | 1 (remaining) | 13           |

**Expected Output:**

* **Last Brick Placed By:** Jack
* **Quantity Placed:** 1 Brick

### Objective

The assignment was designed to strengthen logical thinking by applying loops, conditional statements, variables, and arithmetic operations to solve a real-world programming problem.
