# Day 1 – Introduction to Agentic AI

**Date:** 25 June 2026

## Overview

Today marked the first day of the 6-week Agentic AI Training program. The session began with an introduction to the trainers, participants, and an overview of the course structure. We also discussed the objectives of the training and what we will be building throughout the program.

## Topics Covered

### 1. Introduction to Agentic AI

* Understood the concept of **Agentic AI**.
* Learned how AI agents differ from traditional AI systems.
* Explored how AI agents can autonomously perceive, reason, make decisions, and perform tasks with minimal human intervention.
* Discussed real-world applications of Agentic AI across different industries.

### 2. MVC (Model-View-Controller) Architecture

* Learned the basics of the MVC architectural pattern.
* Understood the responsibilities of each component:

  * **Model:** Manages data and business logic.
  * **View:** Handles the user interface and presentation.
  * **Controller:** Acts as the intermediary between the Model and the View, processing user requests.
* Discussed why MVC helps in building scalable and maintainable applications.

### 3. Program Execution Flow

* Learned how a program interacts with the operating system.
* Understood the role of the OS in executing applications.
* Discussed how different components communicate during program execution, including the interaction between software, the Operating System, and the Control Unit (CU) of the CPU.

## Key Takeaways

* Gained a foundational understanding of Agentic AI and its capabilities.
* Learned the importance of MVC architecture in software development.
* Understood the basic execution flow of a program and its interaction with the operating system.

## Reflection

Day 1 provided a strong conceptual foundation for the training. It introduced the core ideas behind Agentic AI while reinforcing important software engineering concepts like MVC architecture and system interaction. I'm excited to build upon these fundamentals in the coming weeks and apply them to real-world projects.
